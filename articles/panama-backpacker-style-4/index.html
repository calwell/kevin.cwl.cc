Panama Backpacker Style - 4

Day 7 - "The People"
Last night was a little crazy, the Clos wine was a big part of that or "crack in a box" as it would be coined. Tania would get right drunk on that stuff. My experience in the hammock was not all that good either - the locals were really partying and I was being eaten by some sort of painful bugs.. They bit my entire left arm but didn't touch my right arm, weird. Tania woke me up at 8am with coffee and breakfast. I was not ready to wake up and she said "Kev, You have to wake up!" - I thought it was an emergency. When I finally woke up, we were off. There were two couples of Americans down for a road trip that were really cool and let us park our bags in their trailer while we were gone. Since the beach was not going to connect us to the resort (it was right around the point) - we were going to have to take a water taxi or a bus. We chose the bus. After a couple short bus rides we were just outside the Decamron complex. Walking up to the resort on the beach, we met a few of the folks we knew and they would help in the process of obtaining a day pass - that was $54.00. It was Pricey but part of the process if we wanted to stay. We went into the pools , met and hung out with many of the group members - Tim and Christine were nice enough to offer to let me stay the night as well as a hot shower, that was nice! We would all get to the large reservation in the Italian restaurant and party a bit. I turned in early. Our next plan was getting out at 6am and heading to El Valle. Our wrist bags only lasted till 2am and I didn't know for sure where where Tania was staying....

Day 8 - Hello El Valle
6am comes and Tim's alarm goes off. I gather my limited amount of stuff and wait for Tania. After not hearing from her for 40 min I head out to look for her. She ends up being easy to find and we walk out the front door of the resort as inconspicuously as possible. There were a few people that were coming with is on this trip and we were supposed to meet them at the Santa Clara bus stop in a few hours. We went back to the campground, grabbed our bags and then headed up to the bus stop more than an hour early. A long wait to be sure... When they arrive I was surprised to see how many people decided to come out! Jimmy, Don, Dennis and Tom all made the trek. We all grabbed a bus - and I even took my second still picture on this trip to El Valle. This time we would shack up in a small spot called "Cabanas Pitosi", the room had three beds and space for two hammocks outside. Once we were situated we headed for the canopy tour. It was a steep $52.50, but everyone was in and it looked well worth the price. We all hiked up the side of a mountain and then zip-lined back down at three points between the trees, at times close to 180ft off the ground. One line was amazing! We were stopped right above a waterfall halfway across. The view was unbelievable! We also saw rubber trees and a possible Tucan sighting, though he didn't stick around. Dennis and Tom decided to return home and Jimmy, Don, Tania and I headed back to the room, got drunk and slept.

Day 9 - El Valle
So, I was in a hammock last night at "Cabanas Pitosi" - the one noticeable thing being how cold it was - but I slept ok. Everyone else stayed inside the cabin. We woke up to breakfast in bed thanks to Don and Tania walking to the town for us, it was great! More exploring needed to happen today though. We all walked into town and rented bicycles, and then hit the road towards the golden frogs and the square trees. There were very few golden frogs, but the square tress required us to follow this great little "sendero". Along the way we came across these leaf cutter ants.. They were all walking along a path, in one direction carrying pieces of leaves, in the other direction going back, this was incredibly organized. After getting to the square trees we headed to the "pozos de thermale" or thermal pools.

The idea about the thermal pool is that there is water in a basin being heated by the lava of the dormant volcano of El Valle. The water in the pools was indeed incredibly warm to the touch. After paying the $1 entrance fee and seeing the pool - I personally decided to sit it out, it looked pretty nasty. Don, Tania and Jimmy jumped in and seemed to really enjoy it.

By this time Don and Jimmy were considering going back to the resort - Tania did convince them to stay for a drink at the cabins before going. When they decided to leave, it was so late in the town that everything was shut down - the only way to get to the main highway was by taxi, and they were charging $35 to get back to the resort. Somehow, I totally missed the pivotal moment, but the decision was to stay another night...
