Day 10 - Bye El Valle, hello Penonomé

Morning returns and we all have decidedly less pep. No doubt, the drinking had something to do with it. We all straggle up, Don gets milk and we refresh the coffee demon. Packing and bussing ensues as we would head out of El Valle and drop the guys off at the hotel's street in Farillion. Tania and I stay on the bus and continue to a larger town named Penonomé - the capital of the province of Coclé. This town is filled with shops, I even managed to grab a panama flag here - now I have picked up everything I need. There are all sorts of stores named "Peter", Casa Peter, Mr Peter, Bla Bla Peter. At one very unusual moment, I find Internet access while Tania tells me she's going to get fries at Mr Peter. When I finish and go find her, she's nowhere to be found! I take my time and walk up and down the street, I get fries of my own and after about an hour, I decide to head back to the hotel. I was wondering what the hell happened to her. It was a very interesting process trying to explain to the man at the counter in the hotel that I was in room 605 "Seesh, cero, sank-oo" and that I wanted to get into the room without a key. After much sign language, probably a little bit of dancing and poor Spanish, the man with the big ring of keys let me in. I left a note for her and headed out back down the street. It was getting dark so I was getting a bit worried. While walking back into town - Tania was coming out of a mall. She had gone to do her nails. All was fine at this point. We were off to Pio Pio to eat chicken.

Panama 11 - Penonomé
All in all a low-key day in Penonomé... Not much more than shopping and hanging out at the hotel, I managed to walk from one end of the town to the other - the workers were clearly getting a large stage ready for Carnaval.

Panama 12 - Penonomé and Sona
Today, the Carnaval starts... There looked to be no chance to get to Las Tablas so we made the decision we weren't going there. We found some Americans that were willing to let us crash in their "crash pad", twelve of them were in the room next to us. Then, Tania found something better! Professor Ortiz - a local teacher in Penonomé - was willing to rent a room in his house. Sweet. We headed over there and dropped our bags and headed to my MAIN point of interest in Panama - Sona (well known for the location of the key prison in the show Prison Break). The 200km bus ride would cost no more than three bucks, but take a good three hours each way, we needed to get on two different busses. Once in Sona it was clear that this small town had no clue what type of fame has been brought to them, or why. While asking a police officer to take a picture with me, the description of the TV show "Prison Break" elicited deep, blank stares. They would end up saying a forceful no to any sort of pictures. I ended up with a picture at a local bank that said Sona. We stopped at a local chicken place for amazing rotisserie and then jumped on the bus back to Penonomé ...

The bus to Santiago was cool, but the bus from there to Penonomé was horrible! It was impossible to see out the windows to tell where you were - or even to tell if you had gotten to Penonomé , or if you were actually there. Tania asked again and we finally made it to where we were going.

Right when we returned we connected with Kimmie an Jesus be checked out the Carnaval that was going on.. It wasn't busy yet, but the people were coming in to town in droves. I cut the night short because I wasn't feeling so good.. Back to Prof Ortiz house...
