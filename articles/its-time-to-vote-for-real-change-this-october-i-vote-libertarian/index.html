It's time to vote for REAL change. This October I vote Libertarian.

Tue 11 10 2011

[![image][]][image]

Firstly, I want to make it clear, no matter what I say - my hope is that you choose to do what is so important to our way of life. Please vote in this coming Ontario Provincial Election on October 6th, 2011 (click here to find your electoral district) and make your voice heard.

Thank you, now it's time to talk about how I'll vote. In the past Ontario elections I have voted Green party and if I were to go back further, Liberal. This year none of those will get my vote.

This year I vote Liberation.

Liberation? What's that, you say? Is that something to do with the Liberals? No.

The Liber- in Liberation comes from Liberty, not Liberal.

Liberty is:

The state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions imposed by authority on one's way of life, behavior, or political views

For me, the essence of this is that you know what's best for you your family. No other authority can tell you what's best for you. I can't tell you what's best for you.

I really don't know why this isn't more popular. Just a couple days ago, the candidate for the Oshawa Liberation party in Oshawa, Matthew Belanger, was nice enough to meet with me - and I asked him that very question.

"We're just not out there. It's hard to get the word out"

I'll say.

It's more than hard when you're considered what he calls a "fringe" party. You likely won't see the Libertarians in any sort of provincial debate or media platform. Last night's debate only included three parties. What they're talking about is a massive, no, MONSTER shift in policies from where we are now.

Can you imagine completely privatized medicine? Privatized Education?

Can you imagine all roads privatized?

Can you imagine the end of welfare?

Can you imagine the buying and selling beer and wine anywhere?

Can you imagine taxes being a mere fraction of what they are now?

What the Liberation party wants is for the Government to get the hell outof our lives (with the exception of a few things) and let us live the way we see fit. They want to let us keep our money to do what we think is best for ourselves, our family. Imagine a day when you start by getting paid double what you have now (after all, tax freedom day is in june). What would you do with it?

I feel it's time to make huge changes. The oppressively taxed, over bureaucratic system we're a part of today is NOT working. We're part of a culture of too much Government and too much entitlement.

UPDATE - October 11th, 2011 - As it turns out - I live in the WHITBY-OSHAWA riding and there was no Libertarian on the ballot. I was forced to vote another party and vote for what I thought was best. I felt, this time around, that we had very little time to prepare for this vote. The voter turnout was extremely low - Christine Elliott of the PC party was the winner.
