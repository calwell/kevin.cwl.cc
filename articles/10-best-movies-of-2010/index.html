10 Best Movies of 2010

Fri 21 01 2011

[![image][]][image]

Here's the thing, I tend to watch a lot of movies. It may be because, instead of watching TV, I end up watching movies? I'm really not sure why I have always liked watching movies - and even seeing what it takes to makes movies. It's all entertainment and escape at it's best. Each year, I can say I've seen most of what's out there, though some movies (often Oscar Bait-y types) escape my viewing pleasure.

This year, there were a number of great and cool movies that I thought were the best. Some that probably deserve mention, but I haven't seen are Black Swan, True Grit, Enter The Void, The Kids are All Right, King's Speech, and Winter's Bone. For the rest, take a look at what I thought were the best of this year.

[![image][1]][1]

10. Get Him To The Greek (IMDB, Amazon)
The best comedy this year has to be Russell Brand's return as rock star Aldous Snow. It almost seems like he was meant to play this character. Previusly, Brand played this character in another wonderful comedy, Forgetting Sarah Marshall. I really loved the chemistry between Brand and Jonah Hill and the cameos alone were funny as hell. P. Diddy was great here too, who knew he could be funny?. It's mostly stupid and gross comedy, but there is a serious side there too. This is precisely the way I would envision the process of picking up a drugged out rock star.

[![image][3]][3]

9. The Karate Kid (IMDB, Amazon)
I was really not expecting to like this. The little I knew about it (Kung Fu as the fighting style not Karate, relative unknown Jaden Smith, Jackie Chan) - made me think this really was going to be trash. Boy, was I surprised with what I saw! Smith did a great job of acting like a kid with a lot to learn, Jackie Chan wasn't doing his regular jokey shtick here either. The understated nature of nearly everything in this movie makes it come out as a real winner - and wipes thoughts of original out of your mind. The fact that it made a crap load of money probably guarantees a sequel.

[![image][7]][7]

8. Machete (IMDB, Amazon)
I love so much about this film that it's a wonder why it's not higher on my list. Machete is the movie trailer that turned into an actual movie. And that movie is amazing in ways that are unexplainable. Watching Trejo finally getting a leading role and letting him rip shit up like he does. Michelle Rodriguez looks incredible and plays a great hard-ass. If you are a fan of the grind house style movie, this is your movie. And, even thought it didn't make a lot of money it mademoney. I hope to see more of this.

[![image][11]][11]

7. Let Me In (IMDB, Amazon)
Let Me In was another of those surprise movies. When I heard that they were remaking the AMAZING Swedish movie Let the Right One In, I was more than a bit cynical. This seemed too soon for this. What ended up on screen was incredible for a movie that so many were not looking forward too. Most of this rested on the shoulders of Chloe Moretz and I should she did a great job of being strong and venerable all at the same time. Richard Jenkins was great as the father. Do yourself a favor and see both Let the Right One In and this movie.

[![image][15]][15]

6. Buried (IMDB, Amazon)
This movie is a testament to how much can be done with so little. Essentially this is one man, a phone, dirt and a coffin. What Ryan Reynolds does here is make me believe that he's in trouble and imminent danger. The tension in the movie exists right to the last minute with what I'll describe as an unexpected ended. I wasn't expect it, at least. Rodrigo Cortés makes a wonderful, basic and thrilling story, work. As an added bonus, most of the released posers were incredible leading up to the film. This is one that I was looking forward to when I heard about it.

[![image][19]][19]

5. The Social Network (IMDB, Amazon)
Another surprise movie, but it really shouldn't have been a surprise. Look at the talent behind this - director David Fincher and writer Aaron Sorkin. What they made with this movie was more than the technical details (which is often wrong in movies to begin with). They made a movie about the beginnings of an idea - and made that movie at exactly a time when Facebook was in the news. You have great performances by Eisenberg and Timberlake and genuinely interesting characters.

[![image][23]][23]

4. Scott Pilgrim vs The World (IMDB, Amazon)
The movie that practically every geek on the planet was waiting for, finally arrived in 2010. And it was very good. For a movie about a guy from Toronto, shot in Toronto this was a source of great pride to see our wonderful city on display like this. Casa Loma never looked so good! Well, it had me at Toronto. But, so much of everything else about this movie is amazing. From the hyperactive action, to the performances by Cera and Winstead to the sets to, well, everything else. A great movie.

[![image][27]][27]

3. Kick-Ass (IMDB, Amazon)
2010 was definitely the year for Toronto as a location. In this movie, Toronto was the location - but the in-movie city remained unknown. Kick-Ass, of course, was the introduction to super heroes without any super powers. A new kind of comic book movie. And, wow, does it Kick Ass. All of the characters were interesting and flawed and the humor of it all made me laugh a lot. Also Hit Girl is someone you never want to meet in a dark alley.

[![image][31]][31]

2. The Town (IMDB, Amazon)
The one thing you'll be struck by with this second amazing directorial effort by Ben Affleck, is the weight of emotion in all of the characters here. Everyone in this movie is just excellent, from Ben Affleck to Rebecca Hall to Jon Hamm to Jeremy Renner. This is a very character driven movie about a small town in Boston and, frankly, the story just blows you away. Not by how original the story is, but by HOW it's told. I expect Affleck to get an Oscar or three for this one.

[![image][35]][35]

1. Inception (IMDB, Amazon)
Christopher Nolan is a movie-making genius. What he does with story, sound and image is just incredible. Inception is on the top of most people's lists this year because it is one thing - the best movie of 2010. The story is so multi-layered, so interesting, so visual - you'll go back many times and still not fully understand what this all means. A beautiful film in every regard. You'll also love how Nolan has made an effort to use as much real and practical effects as possible - it shows in the final product. I can't wait to see what he does next.

Movies I thought really sucked this year:

Shutter Island (IMDB, Amazon)
This one is moody and well-made and incredibly actied by DiCaprio - but I felt the story was stupid. Really, I saw was was coming a mile away and there was really no suspense to it. You can make garbage look pretty, but it's still garbage right?

The Human Centipede (IMDB, Amazon)
Really, what were they thinking? That's pretty much what was going through my mind every minute of this film. I essentially felt like the woman at the back of the line on this one. Force fed fecal matter of a movie. Brutal.

The Book of Eli (IMDB, Amazon)
The book is a bible. Eli is blind. Really horrible movie.

.. and probably many more that I can't think of. Better to bring in the new year with positivity! Tell me what your favorite move(s) were this year? Ho close is my list to your's? Did I miss anything?
